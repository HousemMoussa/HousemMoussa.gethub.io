AirGULP Drone Project Overview

Mission Overview

AirGULP is a drone designed to assist in research on cormorant populations on Puffin Island, a small land mass off the coast of Anglesey in North Wales. The project aims to achieve three key missions:

  1. Identify the number of cormorant nests during breeding season through image capture.
  2. Locate the positions of these nests using geo-referencing of the stitched images.
  3. Quantify the change in the number of cormorant nests in up to 3 missions over the summer period.

Key Features

  • Portable design for easy transportation
  • Weather-resistant construction
  • High-resolution camera with minimum GSD of 0.82 cm/pixel
  • Ability to reach an altitude of 30m above ground level
  • Automated flight plan capability
  • Manual control option for safety
  • Easily changeable battery pack

Technical Specifications

Airframe

  • Configuration: Quadcopter
  • Material: Fibreglass arms, Polycarbonate legs
  • Landing Gear: Two-legged 'ski' design with rubber dampers
  • Main Hub: IP55 rated electrical box

Propulsion

  • Motors: Velox 2812 925KV
  • Propellers: Carbon Fibre 15x5.5 inch
  • Battery: Spektrum Smart LiPo 7000mAh, 6S, 22.2V, 30C

Avionics

  • Flight Controller: Pixhawk 4
  • GPS Module: Ublox Neo-M8N
  • ESC/PDB: Holybro Tekko32 4-in-1
  • Receiver: FrSky X8R
  • Telemetry: Holybro RF Design 433MHz

Payload

  • Camera: GoPro Hero 9
  • Mount: Ratchet arm for stabilization
